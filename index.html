I am currently a postdoctoral research fellow at Johns Hopkins University. My ongoing research contributes to establishing a closed-loop automated laboratory aimed at accelerating the discovery of novel refractory multi-principal element alloys (RMPEAs) for extreme environments. In parallel, I also work on predicting the thermo-mechanical properties of ice and ice-nucleation mechanisms to identify emerging strategies that could slow the loss of polar ice sheets. I received a BS (2014) and MS (2017) in Mechanical Engineering from K. N. Toosi University of Technology and Sharif University of Technology, respectively, and a PhD in Mechanical Engineering (2023) from the University of Rochester, New York.
