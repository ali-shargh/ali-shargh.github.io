About

I am a postdoctoral research fellow in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Johns Hopkins University, working with Prof. Jaafar El-Awady. My work sits at the intersection of computational mechanics, materials science, and machine learning.

Broadly, I develop physics-informed, data-driven frameworks to understand how microstructural complexity governs the mechanical behavior of materials and to accelerate the discovery of materials for extreme environments, energy systems, and environmental applications.

Research interests

  • AI-driven design of refractory multi-principal element alloys (RMPEAs)
  • Ice-nucleating proteins and mechanics of ice microstructures
  • Mechanically informed design of nanoporous and membrane materials
  • Machine learning surrogates for CALPHAD and atomistic simulations
