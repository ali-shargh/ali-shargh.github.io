I am an AI/ML Research Scientist developing scientific machine learning, uncertainty-aware AI, and autonomous discovery frameworks for scientific and engineering applications. My research focuses on probabilistic machine learning, active learning, generative modeling, and data-driven decision making.

At Johns Hopkins University, I develop AI-driven frameworks for materials discovery and autonomous experimentation, including uncertainty-aware phase prediction models, active-learning systems, and closed-loop discovery workflows. My work has leveraged datasets containing more than 480,000 CALPHAD-labeled material states and explored design spaces exceeding 12 million alloy compositions. This effort is toward establishing a closed-loop automated laboratory (CAIMEE) aimed at accelerating the discovery of novel refractory multi-principal element alloys (RMPEAs) for extreme environments.
